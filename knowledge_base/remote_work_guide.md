# Remote Work Guide

## Setting Up Your Home Office

### Ergonomics
- Position your monitor at eye level, about an arm's length away
- Use a chair with proper lumbar support
- Keep your keyboard and mouse at elbow height
- Take a 5-minute break every hour to stretch and move

### Technology Setup
- Ensure your internet speed is at least 25 Mbps download / 10 Mbps upload
- Use a wired Ethernet connection for video calls when possible
- Set up a backup mobile hotspot for emergencies
- Keep your laptop plugged in during long work sessions

## Communication Best Practices

### Slack Etiquette
- Use threads for detailed discussions
- Set your status to indicate availability
- Respond to direct messages within 2 hours during core hours
- Use @channel sparingly — only for urgent team-wide announcements

### Video Calls
- Test your audio and video before important meetings
- Use a virtual background if your environment is distracting
- Mute yourself when not speaking
- Turn on your camera during team meetings for better engagement

### Asynchronous Communication
- Write clear, self-contained messages that don't require immediate follow-up
- Document decisions in shared notes or wiki pages
- Use Loom for recording walkthroughs instead of scheduling meetings
- Over-communicate context — remote colleagues don't have hallway conversations

## Productivity Tips
- Block focused work time on your calendar (at least 2 hours)
- Use the Pomodoro Technique: 25 minutes of focus, 5 minutes of break
- Keep a daily stand-up log for your own reference
- End each day by writing tomorrow's top 3 priorities
